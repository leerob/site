## Next.js 9.4

[Next.js 9.4](https://nextjs.org/blog/next-9-4) was released with a _ton_ of awesome features. One I'm particularly excited about is [performance reporting](https://nextjs.org/docs/advanced-features/measuring-performance).
I updated my site to try out some of the new features.

I created a dashboard with [Quickmetrics](https://quickmetrics.io/) to track Core Web Vitals over time.
Here's [the code](https://twitter.com/leeerob/status/1259953687784554502/photo/2) if you're curious.

![Next.js 9.4 Performance Metrics](https://buttondown.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/images/1ee11ab7-8449-4879-aa4e-50f09c35bed5.jpeg)

I also tried to [optimize performance](https://twitter.com/leeerob/status/1262067117379158020). The biggest improvement was optimizing my font loading strategy. By preloading & preconnecting to Google Fonts, I saw a notable page load decrease.

![Screenshot of leerob.io](https://buttondown.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/images/2edc880d-69ac-45b5-9f3a-70eda920a091.jpeg)

![Code snippet](https://buttondown.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/images/94cd4a1b-8f81-486e-a428-c8f6dd7dd7a5.png)

**Note:** I tried to switch to Preact, but it broke Fast Refresh. I'm guessing this will be fixed soon. Preact _did_ decrease my page load time even further.
